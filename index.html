Predicting a Heart Attack with Machine Learning

Overview

What We Do

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), heart disease is the leading cause of death in America. Our group wants to spread both awareness and information. With this project, we will have the opportunity to save lives and help others by predicting a heart attack before it’s too late.

Minimum Viable Product

This system will be able to predict the risk of a heart attack based on multiple factors. These factors will be provided through user input. The system will consider these factors to calculate the user’s risk of a heart attack.

Implementation

We experimented with many models in Python, including K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN), Support Vector Machines (SVM), Random Forests, and Logistic Regressions.

Program Timeline

  • Finding Our Data

    Our dataset was supplied by Kaggle.

  • Creating Visualizations

    We used Python as our coding language, with Pandas library for data manipulation and Plotly to create our visualizations.

  • Baseline Machine Learning

    Our machine learning models included K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN), Suport Vector Machine (SVM), Random Forests, and Logistic Regression. With KNN, we saw a baseline accuracy of 78%. SVM gave us a baseline accuracy of 77.1%. Random Forests' baseline accuracy was 83%. Logistic Regression had an initial accuracy of 88%.

  • Standardization and Feature Learning

    With KNN, standardizing the dataset gave us an accuracy of 88%. Standardization on SVM increased accuracy to 86.9%. On Random Forests, standardization decreased accuracy to 81%. We saw similar results with Logistic Regression, where feature engineering lowered the accuracy twice to 86% and 85%.

  • Website Creation

    We learned HTML/CSS to format and create our website, which was deployed on Github.

Conclusion

  • Our highest baseline accuracy was 88.5%, and following standardization, many models also improved and increased their score.
  • Random Forest specifically was improved also by GridSearchCV.
  • Standardization and feature engineering improved the accuracy score for Random Forest and KNN, but decreased the score for SVM and Logistic Regression.
  • Our accuracy may have been improved if we had a larger dataset and performed further hyperparameter tuning or feature engineering.

Sex and Age

How does sex and age relate to risk of a heart attack?

Sex and age are measured by the risk of angiographic disease in a violin plot. Neither age nor sex correlates to risk of a heart attack, as the plot shows that a person of any sex or either sex have similar risks for heart attack.

Heart Attack Factor Correlation

What factors of heart attacks have the most correlation?

This heat map shows the correlation between various factors in heart attacks. Each factor is depicted with the correlation between itself and the other factors. The colour of the square depict correlation shows the level of correlation: a more blue coloured square indicates higher negative correlation, whereas more red coloured squares indicate higher positive correlation. (Yellow squares indicate the factor’s correlation with itself). Oldpeak and slp (the slope of the peak exercise ST segment) have the highest negative correlation and cp (type of chest pain) with the outcome have the highest positive correlation).

Heart Attack Status

Correlation between Heart Attack Status and other health factors

A bar graph was made between the correlation of a patient's’ risk of a heart attack to every other health factor. This was to determine the relevance of specific health factors. Factors with the highest correlation are ranked towards the left. In this data set, exercised induced angina has the highest correlation with a heart attack. Labels have been renamed for clarity.

Chest Pain

How does chest pain effect how much risk a person is at for a heart attack?

We measured the type of chest pain in correlation to the risk of a heart attack. This graph shows that if you have non-anginal pain, atypical angina pain, or if you are asymptomatic you are at a higher risk than people with typical angina pain.

Resting Blood Pressure

How does resting blood pressure effect how much a person is at risk?

We measured the correlation between resting blood pressure and angiographic disease status. This graph shows that there is no correlation between a higher blood pressure and being at a higher risk for a heart attack.
